Basic Theory
Overwatch is a game about team fights, in which a team of 6, split into 2 tanks, two damage heroes, and two supports, fights another team of 6 split into the same ranks. It is the job of the tank to mitigate damage and abilities from the other team in order to create a safe area for their teammates. Damage heroes seek to eliminate and take fights with individuals on the enemy team to give their team an advantage. Supports are the role that constantly enables their teammates through healing and other defensive tools. You can think about it like this: There is a brick wall which is supported by steel braces. The brick wall is being hit repeatedly with a hammer. It is a game of which brick wall breaks first. The brick wall are the tanks, the braces are the supports, and the hammer are the damage. All other things constant, if one brick wall is thicker or stronger, or if the hammer is heavier, or if the braces are of superior steel, that will make the difference. The enjoyable part of the game comes when every single wall, brace, and hammer is different, and you have to adapt to win.
